What is Mental Health and how to deal with your Mental Health!

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It encompasses how individuals think, feel, and behave, as well as how they cope with stress, relate to others, and make choices. Good mental health is essential for functioning well in everyday life, maintaining healthy relationships, and coping with the ups and downs of life.

  • Prioritize self-compassion.
  • Challenge negative thoughts.
  • Seek professional help.

Services

There are various services and resources available to support mental health and well-being. These services can range from preventive measures to crisis intervention. Here are some common services used in mental health:

Therapy/Counseling

Therapy involves talking to a trained mental health professional, such as a psychologist, psychiatrist, counselor, or social worker, to explore thoughts, feelings, and behaviors and to develop coping strategies and solutions for mental health concerns.

Medication Management

Psychiatrists and other medical professionals may prescribe medication to help manage symptoms of mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, or schizophrenia

Online Therapy Platforms

Online therapy platforms offer remote access to therapy and counseling services through video calls, phone calls, or messaging.

Peer Support Program

Peer support programs involve individuals with lived experience of mental health challenges providing support and guidance to others facing similar struggles.

Clients

Mental health clients come from diverse backgrounds and may be seeking support for various reasons, including mood disorders (e.g., depression, bipolar disorder), anxiety disorders, trauma, substance abuse, relationship issues, or other mental health concerns.
